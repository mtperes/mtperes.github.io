Trajectory

Born in 1992, I am a self-taught computer enthusiast who embarked on the digital journey during the late '90s with a Windows 95 operating system running on a Pentium 2, coupled with the iconic dial-up connection. My virtual exploration began by navigating the realms of AOL dial-up software, where I honed my skills in email services and communication through instant messaging and online forums.

Having witnessed the evolution of the internet from its Web 1.0 era to the present day, I actively embraced and utilized a myriad of tools. From hosting images and crafting personal blogs to immersing myself in the learning processes of the web, I delved into magic and honed my communication skills through the practice of illusionism. This multifaceted journey extended to early e-commerce ventures and video content creation.

By the age of 16, I had become a proficient English speaker through a combination of private courses, free conversation classes, and online resources, including gaming and video consumption. The fusion of my magic performances and English fluency opened doors to my first gig—entertaining and educating a large group of kids learning English. This experience led to an invitation to join a teacher training course, leveraging my presentation and explanation skills.

As my trajectory unfolded, I found myself deeply immersed in computer science by the time I turned 21. An opportunity arose when I applied for a government interchange program, granting me a transformative year and a half studying in Canada. This experience ignited a passion for travel, exposing me to diverse lifestyles and perspectives, and broadening my understanding of different concepts. These experiences collectively shaped my multifaceted journey, blending technology, language, and the art of illusion into a unique and evolving narrative.
